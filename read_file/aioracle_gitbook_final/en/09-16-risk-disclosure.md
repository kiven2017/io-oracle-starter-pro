# 16. Risk Disclosure

## 16.1 Purpose
This chapter discloses, in a prudent and transparent manner, the technical, device, node, market, commercial, compliance, and governance risks that AIOracle may face.

## 16.2 Technical Risks
These include protocol and smart contract vulnerabilities, AI inference uncertainty, cross-chain and bridging risks, and service interruption or security incidents arising from third-party dependencies.

## 16.3 Data and Device Risks
These include hardware failure, false data, data pollution, privacy risk, and insufficient data-governance controls.

## 16.4 Node and Network Risks
These include node concentration, lazy evaluation, insufficient service quality, and imbalance in node economics.

## 16.5 Market and Token Risks
These include $AIO price volatility, inadequate liquidity, and tokenomics execution that may differ from current expectations.

## 16.6 Commercial and Ecosystem Risks
These include delays in adoption, changes in ecosystem partnerships, and stronger competition.

## 16.7 Legal and Regulatory Risks
Blockchain, AI, data governance, and digital-asset regulations may evolve differently across jurisdictions.

## 16.8 Forward-Looking Statements
Future plans described in the whitepaper are forward-looking and do not constitute guarantees of listing, returns, or specific market outcomes.
